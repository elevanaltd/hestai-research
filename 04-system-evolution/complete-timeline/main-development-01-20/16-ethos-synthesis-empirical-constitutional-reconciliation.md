### Option 3: Strict Constitutional Enforcement (Reject Empirical Evidence)

## Human Operator Intervention

*At this point, the human operator asked: "Isn't there a 4th & 5th option? 1) Have a separate shaft for conceptual and actual, or 2) move those restrictions into guidelines?"*

*This intervention opened new pathways that ETHOS then validated:*

### Option 4: Separate SHAFTs for Conceptual and Actual Phases

**Mechanism**
- **CONCEPTUAL SHAFT**: Strictly enforces role separation—PATHOS only explores, LOGOS only synthesizes, ETHOS only validates. No implementation rights for PATHOS.
- **ACTUAL SHAFT**: New or modified role boundaries—explicitly authorizes PATHOS to build, LOGOS to specify, ETHOS to validate, for the L→E→P phase only.
- **Handoff Protocol**: Transition from CONCEPTUAL to ACTUAL must be signaled by a "phase switch" event, with both SHAFTs loaded and enforced appropriately for that context.

**Implications**
- Maximum clarity: Each phase has its own constitutional anchor—no ambiguity about permitted actions in either domain.
- Systemic safety: Role drift in one phase cannot bleed into the other.
- Flexible governance: Evolution in one phase doesn't force change on the other.

**Requirements**
- Versioned SHAFT documents per phase.
- Explicit phase change logging and enforcement mechanism.
- ETHOS review at every phase transition.

### Option 5: Move Certain Restrictions from Constitutional to Guideline Status

**Mechanism**
- Rewrite critical role boundaries as guidelines rather than absolute law in SHAFT (or create a new "Operational Guidelines" layer beneath SHAFT).
- **Guidelines**: Strongly preferred behaviors, violation triggers warnings/escalations but not hard prevention.
- **Constitution**: Only the minimum set of constraints necessary to maintain system safety and identity; everything else is best-practice guidance.

**Implications**
- Enables empirical evolution: If empirical results prove a guideline suboptimal, the system can adjust behavior with minimal friction.
- ETHOS becomes advisor, not just enforcer: Surfaces risks and warnings instead of halting work.
- System can adapt in real time: Allows for exceptions when strongly justified by empirical evidence.

**Requirements**
- Refactoring of SHAFT, Odyssean Anchor, and enforcement logic to distinguish hard boundaries vs guidelines.
- ETHOS escalation for repeated or high-risk guideline violations.
- Audit trail for guideline overrides (for pattern learning and future governance refinement).
